# Examples

This section illustrates how the components and interaction patterns described
in this stylebook are used together in real checkout flows.

---

## Card selection

Cards are used consistently to present selectable payment methods.
Each card includes an icon, title, and optional description.

![Payment method selection][payment-method-selection-img]{: style="max-width:400px"}

---

## Expanded card

When a card is selected, the card expands to reveal its content.

![Expanded card][expanded-card-img]{: style="max-width:400px"}

---

## Selection in lists

Only one item can be selected at a time. The selected item is clearly
distinguishable using borders and icons.

![Selection in lists][selection-in-lists-img]{: style="max-width:400px"}

---

## Complex selection in lists

More complex selection lists use the same card and selection patterns.

![Complex payment options][complex-payment-options-img]{: style="max-width:400px"}

---

## Validation success

Success states are communicated using multiple cues:
border and icon. No additional text is required.

![Validation success][validation-success-example-img]{: style="max-width:400px"}

---

## Validation error

Error states are communicated using multiple cues:
border, icon, and explanatory text.

![Validation error][validation-error-example-img]{: style="max-width:400px"}
